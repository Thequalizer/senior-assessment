Decision Timeline
Scroll — the active step highlights automatically.
  1. Situation
    Headless transition planning started to slow down — dependencies were not clearly owned and delivery confidence dropped.
  2. Problem
    “Mini components” lived in a shared repository owned by another provider, blocking reliable estimates and planning for our migrations.
  3. Risk Identified
    The number of missing mini components would compound delays. The longer we waited, the more migrations would be blocked.
  4. Analysis
    Mini components were strategically valuable because they integrate the design system via tokens — skipping them would reduce long-term scalability.
  5. Options & Trade-offs
    Option 1: our team develops missing mini components (removes dependency bottleneck).
    Option 2: migrate without mini components (faster short-term, weaker design system alignment).
  6. Alignment & Decision
    I aligned PM/BA/PO and drove the technical narrative in the decision meeting — securing buy-in to take ownership of missing mini components.
  7. Outcome & Impact
    We expanded scope responsibly at the right moment. Result: migration was unblocked and the feature team was not downsized during resourcing pressure.
